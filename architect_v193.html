üìñ User Guide
1. The Evidence Lab (Student Mode)
  • View Text: Click the "üìÑ View Text" button (top right) to read the original source passage.
  • Step 1: Select a colored quote card from the "Evidence" grid.
  • Step 2 (Match): Choose the correct meaning of the quote.
  • Step 3 (Analyze): Choose a Lens (Tone, Structure, or Language). Note: Teachers can hide specific lenses if they are empty or irrelevant for a quote.
  • Whole Text: The gray card at the bottom covers general structure questions.
2. The Writing Desk
  • Sentence Flow: The list now shows exactly where your chosen quote fits (in the blue box).
  • Build: Use the dropdown menus to construct a high-level analytical sentence.
  • Manual Entry: If a box asks you to "Write your own...", type your own idea in the text box.
  • Save: Click "Add to Answer" to move your sentence into the main essay box.
3. Teacher Tools (Settings)
  • Assign Levels: In "Edit Evidence", check [L1], [L2], or [L3] to decide which quotes appear at which level. Use this to separate easy/hard quotes.
  • AI Generator: Paste text/question, generate prompt, and load JSON. Use "Append" to add to existing lessons.
  • Manual Slots: In "Sentence Structure (Admin)", set any slot to "Manual (Starter)", "Manual (Link)", etc. to give students specific writing prompts.
  • Worksheet: Generates a printable Word doc. "Manual" slots appear as labeled blank lines.
